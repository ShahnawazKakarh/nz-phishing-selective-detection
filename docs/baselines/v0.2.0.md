## Cross-model comparison — `source=nz_synth`

All three models score 1.000 F1. **Ceiling effect persists.** This invalidates
any NZ-features-vs-ablation conclusion on the current synthetic corpus and is
the primary motivator for v0.2.1.

## Honest findings (carried over and updated)

1. **Synthetic NZ saturation remains the bottleneck.** Adding a stronger
   classifier family (DistilBERT) did not break the ceiling — confirming the
   issue is with the *dataset*, not classifier capacity.
2. **DistilBERT is a marginal aggregate winner but expensive.** 1,000× more
   compute (329 s vs 0.3 s) for a ~0.5 pt F1 gain on the dominant subgroup.
   This trade-off matters for the eventual deferral analysis.
3. **All models near-perfect on email channel (n=37).** The email test subset
   is too thin to support per-channel claims. Email needs to grow substantially
   in v0.2.1.


## v0.2.1 plan (breaks the synthetic ceiling)

1. **LLM-paraphrased NZ lures.** Use Gemini 2.5 Flash to rewrite every template
   into 5–10 paraphrases so TF-IDF n-grams can no longer memorize them. Same
   four NZ lure families (IRD, NZ Post, Waka Kotahi, banks).
2. **Adversarial NZ negatives.** Legitimate-looking NZ-flavored ham that
   *mentions* IRD, NZ Post, or banks — to penalize models that flag any NZ
   entity as phishing.
3. **Email corpus expansion.** Filter Nazario phishing emails for NZ-relevant
   brands and add SpamAssassin ham as legitimate-class email volume.
4. **Gemini 2.5 Flash LLM-as-classifier.** Zero-shot + 4-shot, with the
   disk-cache + quota-resilient pattern from
   `hate-speech-detection v0.2.1`.

After v0.2.1, the NZ-features-vs-ablation comparison should produce a
measurable gap, and the three-classifier-family benchmark (linear, transformer,
LLM) becomes a publishable contribution.
